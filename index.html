What Is Email List Growth Rate?

Your email list growth rate measures how fast your subscriber base is expanding after accounting for unsubscribes. A growing list means your content and lead magnets are attracting new contacts faster than you are losing them.

The formula:

Growth Rate = ((New Subscribers − Unsubscribes) ÷ Starting Subscribers) × 100

AU Benchmarks by Growth Stage

There is no single right growth rate — it depends on your business model and how actively you are promoting your list. That said, here are typical ranges for Australian SMBs:

Scenario Monthly Growth Rate
Healthy growth (active list building) 2–5%
Moderate growth (some promotion) 1–2%
Flat (maintenance mode) 0–1%
Negative growth (list decay) < 0%

If your list is shrinking month-on-month, that is a signal to audit your content relevance, opt-in sources, or sending frequency before your list becomes too small to market to effectively.

How to Improve Your List Growth Rate

  • Add a lead magnet: A free resource (checklist, guide, calculator) in exchange for an email address consistently outperforms a generic newsletter subscribe prompt.
  • Reduce unsubscribes: Segment your list and send content relevant to each segment. Irrelevant email is the number one reason people unsubscribe.
  • Promote your list actively: Your website, social profiles, and email signature should all include a clear opt-in prompt.
  • Check your opt-in sources: Which channels are sending you subscribers? Double down on what is working and cut what is not.
  • Review your welcome sequence: A strong welcome email series reduces early churn and sets expectations for new subscribers from day one.

Australian businesses that consistently grow their list at 2–5% per month double their subscriber base roughly every 15–36 months without any paid advertising — purely through organic content and system-driven opt-ins.
